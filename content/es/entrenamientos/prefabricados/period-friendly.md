## El Entrenamiento

Este gentle flow is designed to ease period discomfort while still giving you movement. We focus on hip opening, gentle stretching, and positions that can help relieve cramps.

### Centearo (2 minutos)

**Supported Reclined Position**
- Lie back with knees bent
- Place a pillow under your lower back if desired
- Hands on belly
- Deep belly breaths - inhale expand, exhale soften
- 10 breaths

### Hip Openers (5 minutos)

**Supine Butterfly (2 min)**
- Soles of feet together
- Knees fall open
- Hands on inner thighs for gentle pressure
- Breathe and relax

**Happy Baby (1.5 min)**
- Hold outside edges of feet
- Knees toward armpits
- Rock gently side to side
- Release hip tension

**Supine Figure-4 (1.5 min)**
- Ankle on opposite knee
- Draw legs toward chest
- 45 seconds each side

### Gentle Movement (5 minutos)

**Cat-Cow (2 min)**
- Very slow and gentle
- Let your belly hang in cow
- Really round in cat
- 10 slow cycles

**Child's Pose with Wide Knees (1.5 min)**
- Knees wide apart
- Arms extended or by sides
- Rest forehead on colchoneta or pillow
- Breathe into lower back

**Thread the Needle (1.5 min)**
- From all fours
- Thread arm under
- Rest shoulder down
- 45 seconds each side

### Lower Back Release (3 minutos)

**Knee Circles**
- On back, knees to chest
- Circle knees together
- 10 circles each direction

**Supine Twist**
- Knees to one side
- Arms out
- Look opposite direction
- 1 minuto each side

## Poses That Help Cramps

These positions can help relieve menstrual cramps:

1. **Child's Pose** - Compresses and relaxes the abdomen
2. **Supine Twist** - Gently massages internal organs
3. **Cat-Cow** - Increases blood flow to pelvis
4. **Happy Baby** - Releases lower back and hips
5. **Legs Up the Wall** - Reduces pelvic congestion

## What to Skip Duaro Your Period

Listen to your body, but consider avoiding:
- Intense core work (can increase cramping)
- Inversions if you have heavy flow
- Anything that feels uncomfortable
- High-intensity cardio if you're fatigued

## Self-Care Additions

- Use a heating pad before or after
- Drink ginger or peppermint tea
- Keep the room warm
- Use a lavender pillow for relaxation

## Listen to Your Body

Every cycle is different. Some days you might feel like doing more, other days just the gentle stretches. Both are okay. Movement duaro your period can actually help reduce symptoms, but rest is also important.
