## Carrier of the Trier Legacy

Deborah Lessen represents a unique lineage in the Pilates world. She trained with Carola Trier - herself a student of Joseph Pilates - for nearly 18 years, making her one of the few living links to this important branch of Pilates history.

## Training with Carola Trier

Carola Trier was one of Joseph Pilates' earliest students, opening the first Pilates studio outside of his own in 1960. Deborah's nearly two decades of study with Carola gave her deep understanding of:

- Carola's unique teaching style
- Exercises and modifications specific to the Trier approach
- The lineage's emphasis on rehabilitation
- Working with dancers and performers

## Greene Street Studio

Deborah founded Greene Street Studio in New York City, where she has:

- Maintained Carola Trier's teaching methods
- Trained new generations of instructors
- Worked with dancers, athletes, and rehabilitation clients
- Preserved this unique Pilates lineage

## The Trier Approach

Carola Trier's method, which Deborah preserves, is distinguished by:

### Emphasis on Rehabilitation
Carola worked extensively with people recovering from injuries, and this rehabilitation focus remains central.

### Integration with Dance
As dancers, both Carola and Deborah brought artistic sensibility to Pilates.

### Unique Exercises
Some exercises and modifications are specific to the Trier lineage.

### Individual Focus
Working with each client's unique needs and limitations.

## Teaching Philosophy

> "Pilates is about understanding your own body and working with it, not against it."

Deborah's approach emphasizes:

- **Individual assessment** - Understanding each body
- **Rehabilitation principles** - Working with limitations
- **Classical foundation** - Honoring the original method
- **Artistic expression** - Movement as art
