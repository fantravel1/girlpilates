## The Science of Movement

Elizabeth Larkam is one of the most intellectually curious and innovative voices in contemporary Pilates. She has spent decades exploring the intersection of Pilates, physical therapy, neuroscience, and fascia research.

## Background

Elizabeth studied dance and began her Pilates journey studying with first-generation teachers. She worked extensively in rehabilitation settings, which shaped her understanding of how movement can heal.

## Innovations and Contributions

### Fascia-Informed Movement

Elizabeth has been a pioneer in applying fascia research to Pilates:

- Understanding how fascial networks affect movement
- Developing exercises that address fascial continuities
- Teaching practitioners to "think fascia"
- Integrating Tom Myers' Anatomy Trains concepts

### Neuroscience Integration

Her work incorporates understanding of:

- Motor learning and neuroplasticity
- Movement variability for brain health
- Cognitive aspects of exercise
- How the nervous system controls movement

### Movement Cueing

Elizabeth is known for innovative, imaginative cueing that helps clients understand and feel movement:

- Metaphorical imagery
- Sensory-based instructions
- Three-dimensional awareness
- Playful exploration

## Balanced Body Master Instructor

As a key member of Balanced Body's faculty, Elizabeth has:

- Developed continuing education courses
- Created workshop curricula
- Influenced equipment design
- Trained instructors worldwide

## Publications and Presentations

Elizabeth has contributed to the field through:

- "Fascia in Motion" - Comprehensive course on fascia and movement
- Numerous articles and papers
- International conference presentations
- DVD and online educational content

## Teaching Philosophy

> "The brain changes the body. The body changes the brain. Pilates is the perfect vehicle for this dialogue."

Elizabeth emphasizes:

- **Curiosity** - Always questioning and exploring
- **Variability** - Not repeating the same patterns
- **Integration** - Connecting parts to the whole
- **Play** - Movement should be exploratory

## Influence

Elizabeth's work has influenced how contemporary Pilates addresses:

- Chronic pain
- Movement disorders
- Aging populations
- Athletic performance
- Overall wellness

## Legacy

Through her teaching, writing, and presenting, Elizabeth Larkam has:

- Elevated the intellectual discourse in Pilates
- Bridged Pilates with scientific research
- Introduced fascia awareness to movement professionals
- Inspired practitioners to keep learning

Her contribution reminds us that Pilates is a living, evolving method that can incorporate new understanding while honoring its roots.
