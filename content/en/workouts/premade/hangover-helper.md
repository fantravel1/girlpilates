## Before You Start

- Drink a full glass of water
- Have some crackers or toast if your stomach allows
- Keep water nearby
- Move very slowly
- It's okay to skip any movement that doesn't feel good

## The Workout

These are the gentlest possible movements to help you feel better. Nothing intense, no pressure.

### In Bed (5 minutes)

**Full Body Stretch**
- Stretch arms overhead
- Point toes
- Make yourself as long as possible
- Hold 10 seconds, release
- 3 times

**Knee Hugs**
- Hug knees to chest
- Rock gently side to side
- 1 minute
- Massages lower back

**Supine Twist**
- Knees to one side
- Arms out
- 1 minute each side
- Helps with digestion

**Happy Baby**
- If your stomach can handle it
- Hold outside of feet
- Knees toward armpits
- Very gentle rocking
- Skip if nauseous

### Sitting Up (5 minutes)

**Seated Breathing**
- Sit up slowly
- Take 10 deep breaths
- Exhale longer than inhale
- This helps your nervous system

**Neck Circles**
- Very slow and gentle
- 5 circles each direction
- Don't drop head back if dizzy

**Seated Cat-Cow**
- Hands on knees
- Round and arch slowly
- 10 gentle cycles

**Seated Side Stretch**
- Reach one arm up and over
- 30 seconds each side

### Standing (Optional - 5 minutes)

Only do this section if you feel ready to stand

**Standing with Support**
- Hold onto something stable
- March in place gently
- 1 minute

**Wall Forward Fold**
- Hands on wall
- Walk back, fold from hips
- Let head hang
- 1 minute

**Gentle Walk**
- Walk around your space
- Moving helps your body process
- 3 minutes

## Recovery Additions

What else helps:
- More water (obviously)
- Electrolytes or coconut water
- Gentle food when ready
- Fresh air
- Sleep if possible
- Time (unfortunately)

## Why Movement Helps

- Increases circulation
- Helps your liver function
- Releases tension from poor sleep position
- Gentle movement helps nausea more than lying still
- Gets oxygen flowing to your brain

## What to Avoid

- Anything intense
- Inversions (head below heart)
- Fast movements
- Jumping
- Holding breath
- Being hard on yourself
