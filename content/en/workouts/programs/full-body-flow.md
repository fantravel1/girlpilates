## Overview

This balanced 30-minute practice works your entire body while flowing smoothly from one exercise to the next. You'll strengthen, stretch, and feel amazing.

## The Workout

### Warm-Up (4 minutes)

**Breathing (1 min)**
- Lateral breathing, 10 deep breaths

**Pelvic Tilts (1 min)**
- 10 slow, controlled reps

**Hip Circles (1 min)**
- Knees to chest, circle knees
- 5 each direction

**Chest Lift (1 min)**
- 10 reps with control

### Core Series (8 minutes)

**The Hundred (1.5 min)**
- Full 100 pumps

**Roll Up (1.5 min)**
- 6-8 smooth roll ups

**Rolling Like a Ball (1 min)**
- 8 rolls, finding balance

**Single Leg Stretch (1 min)**
- 10 each side

**Double Leg Stretch (1 min)**
- 8 reps

**Criss-Cross (1 min)**
- 8 each side

**Spine Stretch Forward (1 min)**
- 5 stretches

### Side-Lying Series (6 minutes)

**Side Leg Lifts (1 min each side)**
- 15 controlled lifts per side

**Side Leg Circles (1 min each side)**
- 8 each direction per side

**Inner Thigh Lifts (1 min each side)**
- 15 lifts per side

### Back Strengthening (5 minutes)

**Swimming (1.5 min)**
- 3 sets of 20 counts

**Swan Prep (1.5 min)**
- 8 lifts with control

**Child's Pose (30 sec)**
- Rest and reset

**Opposite Arm/Leg Reach (1.5 min)**
- 10 each side, hold 3 counts

### Standing/Kneeling (4 minutes)

**Cat-Cow (1 min)**
- 8 slow cycles

**Bird Dog (1.5 min)**
- 8 each side, hold 5 counts

**Kneeling Side Kick (1.5 min)**
- 8 kicks each side

### Cool-Down (3 minutes)

**Seated Spine Twist (1 min)**
- 5 each side

**Mermaid Stretch (1 min)**
- 4 each side

**Roll Down to Standing (30 sec)**

**Standing Chest Opener (30 sec)**
- Clasp hands behind back, lift chest

## Flow Tips

- Let one exercise flow into the next
- Maintain core connection throughout
- Match movement to breath
- Keep energy consistent - not too fast, not too slow

## Modifications

**Beginner adjustments:**
- Take more rest between sections
- Keep legs bent during core work
- Reduce reps as needed

**Advanced options:**
- Add transitions between exercises
- Increase hold times
- Add small weights for arm work
