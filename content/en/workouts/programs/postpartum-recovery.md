## Important Notice

Wait until 6+ weeks postpartum (8+ weeks for C-section) and get clearance from your healthcare provider before starting this or any exercise program.

## Overview

This gentle routine focuses on reconnecting with your deep core muscles and restoring pelvic floor function. It's specifically designed for the postpartum body and addresses common concerns like diastasis recti.

## The Workout

### Breathing Reconnection (4 minutes)

**Diaphragmatic Breathing (2 min)**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Hands on lower belly
- Inhale: belly gently rises
- Exhale: belly gently falls
- 10 deep breaths

**360 Breathing (2 min)**
- Hands on sides of ribcage
- Feel ribs expand sideways on inhale
- Feel ribs come in on exhale
- Reconnect breath to core
- 10 breaths

### Pelvic Floor Series (4 minutes)

**Gentle Kegels (1.5 min)**
- Exhale: gently lift pelvic floor
- Inhale: release completely
- Light engagement only
- 10 reps

**Elevator Kegels (1.5 min)**
- Imagine floors of a building
- Exhale: lift to floor 1
- Continue lifting to floor 2, 3
- Release down floor by floor
- 5 cycles

**Connection Breath (1 min)**
- Exhale: gentle Kegel + gentle core engagement
- Inhale: full release
- Feel the coordination
- 10 reps

### Gentle Core Activation (5 minutes)

**Heel Slides (1.5 min)**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Exhale: slide one heel away
- Inhale: slide back
- Keep pelvis stable
- 8 each leg

**Toe Taps (1.5 min)**
- Knees at 90 degrees
- Exhale: lower one toe to floor
- Inhale: return
- Maintain neutral spine
- 8 each leg

**Leg Lifts (2 min)**
- One leg at a time
- Exhale: lift bent leg
- Inhale: lower with control
- 8 each leg

### Gentle Stretching (2 minutes)

**Knee Drops (1 min)**
- Gentle twist side to side
- 5 each side

**Child's Pose (1 min)**
- Wide knees
- Rest and breathe

## Diastasis Recti Awareness

**To Check:**
- Lie on back, knees bent
- Place fingers above belly button
- Lift head slightly
- Feel for gap between muscles

**If Present:**
- All exercises in this workout are safe
- Focus on exhale with any effort
- Avoid traditional crunches
- Progress slowly

## Progression

**Weeks 6-8:** This workout, 2-3x per week
**Weeks 8-12:** Add gentle walking, increase reps
**Weeks 12+:** Gradually progress to beginner workouts

## Listen to Your Body

- Stop if you feel pain or pressure
- Stop if you experience leaking
- Rest when you need to
- Progress slowly - you just did something amazing!
