## Overview

This quick morning routine wakes up your entire body, gets your blood flowing, and prepares you for the day ahead. No equipment needed - just roll out of bed and onto your mat.

## The Workout

### Warm-Up (2 minutes)

**Breathing (1 min)**
- Lie on your back, knees bent
- Place hands on ribcage
- Take 5 deep lateral breaths
- Feel your body wake up

**Pelvic Tilts (1 min)**
- Keep same position
- Exhale, tilt pelvis and flatten lower back
- Inhale, return to neutral
- 10 repetitions, slow and controlled

### Main Workout (7 minutes)

**Chest Lift (1 min)**
- Hands behind head
- Exhale, curl head and shoulders up
- Inhale, lower with control
- 10 reps

**Single Leg Stretch (1.5 min)**
- Curl up, hold one knee to chest
- Extend other leg 45 degrees
- Switch legs with rhythm
- 20 switches (10 each side)

**Bridge (1 min)**
- Feet flat, arms by sides
- Exhale, peel spine off mat one vertebra at a time
- Inhale at top
- Exhale, roll down slowly
- 8 reps

**Spine Twist Supine (1 min)**
- Arms out to sides, knees to chest
- Let knees fall to one side
- Look opposite direction
- Switch sides
- 6 each side

**Swimming Prep (1 min)**
- Roll to stomach
- Arms extended forward
- Lift opposite arm and leg
- Alternate
- 16 total

**Cat-Cow (1.5 min)**
- Come to all fours
- Exhale, round spine up (cat)
- Inhale, arch and look up (cow)
- 10 cycles

### Cool-Down (1 minute)

**Child's Pose (30 sec)**
- Sit back onto heels
- Arms extended forward
- Breathe deeply

**Standing Roll Down (30 sec)**
- Stand tall
- Roll down one vertebra at a time
- Hang at bottom
- Roll up slowly
- Ready for your day!

## Tips for Success

- Do this routine before coffee for maximum energy benefit
- Keep movements gentle - your body is just waking up
- Focus on breath to oxygenate your system
- Consistency matters more than perfection

## Modifications

**If your back is stiff in the morning:**
- Spend extra time on pelvic tilts
- Keep legs bent higher during single leg stretch
- Make smaller movements

**To increase challenge:**
- Add 5 more reps to each exercise
- Hold positions for 2-3 breaths
- Move through exercises with less rest
