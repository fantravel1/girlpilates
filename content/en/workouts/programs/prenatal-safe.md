## Important Notice

Always consult your healthcare provider before starting any exercise program during pregnancy. Stop immediately if you experience pain, dizziness, or any concerning symptoms.

## Overview

This gentle workout helps maintain core connection, pelvic floor function, and overall strength during pregnancy. All exercises are modified for safety and comfort.

## The Workout

### Breathing & Connection (3 minutes)

**Seated Breathing (1.5 min)**
- Sit comfortably on mat or cushion
- Hands on belly
- Deep diaphragmatic breaths
- Feel baby move with your breath

**Pelvic Floor Connection (1.5 min)**
- Gentle Kegel exercises
- Lift and release pelvic floor
- 10 reps, gentle holds
- Don't hold breath

### Seated Warm-Up (4 minutes)

**Neck Rolls (1 min)**
- Gentle circles
- 3 each direction

**Shoulder Rolls (1 min)**
- Roll back and down
- 10 reps

**Seated Cat-Cow (1 min)**
- Hands on knees
- Gentle arch and round
- 8 cycles

**Seated Side Stretch (1 min)**
- Reach one arm overhead
- Gentle side bend
- 30 seconds each side

### All Fours Series (5 minutes)

**Cat-Cow (1.5 min)**
- On hands and knees
- Arch and round gently
- 10 cycles

**Bird Dog Modified (2 min)**
- Extend opposite arm and leg
- Keep low, controlled movements
- 8 each side

**Hip Circles (1.5 min)**
- Circle hips gently
- 8 each direction
- Relieves lower back

### Side-Lying Series (5 minutes)

**Clamshells (1.5 min)**
- Knees bent, hips stacked
- Open and close gently
- 12 each side

**Side Leg Lifts (1.5 min)**
- Gentle lifts, not too high
- 10 each side

**Inner Thigh Lifts (2 min)**
- Top leg forward for support
- Lift bottom leg
- 10 each side

### Standing Series (3 minutes)

**Wall Push-Ups (1 min)**
- Stand arm's length from wall
- 10 gentle push-ups

**Calf Raises (1 min)**
- Hold wall for balance
- Rise and lower slowly
- 15 reps

**Squats (1 min)**
- Wide stance
- Gentle depth
- 10 reps

## Modifications by Trimester

**First Trimester:**
- Listen to your body
- Avoid overheating
- Stay hydrated

**Second Trimester:**
- Avoid lying flat on back for extended periods
- Use pillows for support
- Reduce range of motion as needed

**Third Trimester:**
- Focus on gentle movement
- Keep standing work near wall for balance
- Prioritize comfort over performance

## Safety Guidelines

- Stay hydrated
- Avoid overheating
- No lying flat on back after first trimester
- Stop if anything feels wrong
- Keep intensity moderate
- Listen to your body always
