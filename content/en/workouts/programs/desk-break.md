## Overview

Sitting at a desk all day wreaks havoc on your body. This quick routine can be done at your desk (or anywhere) to release tension and reset your posture. No mat or special clothing needed.

## The Workout

### Seated Series (5 minutes)

**Seated Breathing (30 sec)**
- Sit tall, feet flat
- 5 deep lateral breaths
- Reset your posture

**Neck Rolls (1 min)**
- Drop chin to chest
- Slowly roll ear to shoulder
- Roll to other side
- 3 full circles each direction

**Shoulder Rolls (30 sec)**
- Roll shoulders up, back, down
- 8 full rolls backward

**Seated Cat-Cow (1 min)**
- Hands on knees
- Round spine, look down
- Arch spine, look up
- 8 cycles

**Seated Spine Twist (1 min)**
- Cross right leg over left
- Twist toward right
- Hold 20 seconds
- Switch sides

**Seated Figure-4 (1 min)**
- Ankle on opposite knee
- Lean forward slightly
- 30 seconds each side

### Standing Series (5 minutes)

**Standing Roll Down (1 min)**
- Stand tall
- Roll down vertebra by vertebra
- Hang at bottom, shake head "no"
- Roll up slowly
- 2 reps

**Chest Opener (30 sec)**
- Clasp hands behind back
- Lift chest, squeeze shoulder blades
- Hold 20 seconds

**Wall Angels (1 min)**
- Back against wall
- Arms in "goalpost" position
- Slide arms up and down
- 8 reps

**Standing Hip Flexor Stretch (1 min)**
- Step one foot back
- Tuck pelvis, lean forward
- 30 seconds each side

**Calf Raises (30 sec)**
- Rise onto toes
- Lower with control
- 15 reps

**Final Tall Stand (1 min)**
- Stand tall
- Imagine string pulling crown up
- Take 3 deep breaths
- Return to work refreshed!

## Do This When:

- Every 1-2 hours of sitting
- Before/after meetings
- When you feel tension building
- Anytime you need a mental break
- While waiting for files to download

## Office-Friendly Tips

- No one needs to know you're exercising
- Most stretches look like normal movements
- Close your office door if self-conscious
- Set a reminder on your calendar
