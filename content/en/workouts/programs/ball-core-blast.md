## Ball Core Blast

This focused workout uses the stability ball to take your core training to the next level. The unstable surface forces deeper muscle activation than mat work alone.

### What You'll Need
- Stability ball (properly sized for your height)
- Exercise mat
- Water bottle

### Warm-Up (3 minutes)

1. **Seated Bounces** - Sit on ball and gently bounce (1 min)
2. **Seated Hip Circles** - Circle hips in both directions (1 min)
3. **Spinal Waves** - Roll through spine while seated (1 min)

### Main Workout (20 minutes)

#### Block 1: Core Stability
- **Ball Plank** - Hands on ball, hold 30 sec x 3
- **Ball Dead Bug** - 10 reps each side
- **Ball Knee Tucks** - 12 reps x 2

*Rest 30 seconds*

#### Block 2: Rotational Core
- **Ball Russian Twist** - 15 reps each side
- **Ball Oblique Crunch** - 12 reps each side x 2
- **Ball Pendulum** - 10 reps each direction

*Rest 30 seconds*

#### Block 3: Back & Core Integration
- **Ball Back Extension** - 12 reps x 2
- **Ball Superman** - 10 reps x 2
- **Ball Bridge** - 15 reps, hold last rep 10 sec

*Rest 30 seconds*

#### Block 4: Advanced Core
- **Ball Pike** - 8 reps x 2
- **Ball Pass** - 10 reps x 2
- **Ball Reverse Crunch** - 12 reps x 2

### Cool-Down (2 minutes)

1. **Ball Chest Opener** - Drape back over ball, arms wide (30 sec)
2. **Ball Mermaid Stretch** - Each side (30 sec per side)
3. **Ball Cat-Cow** - Gentle spinal movement (30 sec)

### Modifications

**Make it Easier:**
- Use a wall for support during planks
- Reduce reps by half
- Take longer rest breaks

**Make it Harder:**
- Add a pause at the most challenging point
- Slow down the tempo
- Reduce rest to 15 seconds
